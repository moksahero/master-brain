# Monthly Optimization Roadmap

The monthly optimization roadmap turns the [[Channel Health Audit]] into a
short,
prioritized plan of experiments and changes for the next month. It exists to
prevent the two
common failure modes: changing everything at once, which makes results
unreadable, and
changing nothing, which lets problems compound. The roadmap commits to a small
number of
tests with clear hypotheses and a rollback note.

## Inputs

- The latest [[Channel Health Audit]] scorecard and its single weakest link.
- The packaging A/B candidate list from the audit.
- The current [[Source Intake and Refresh]] facts.

## Steps

1. Pick one bottleneck. From the audit, choose the single weakest link in the
   funnel as the month's focus.
2. Set hypotheses. For each planned change, write what you expect to move and
   why, tied to a concept note.
3. Queue packaging tests. Start a [[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]] on the
   flagged candidates; the winner is decided by watch time share.
4. Plan production. Apply [[Packaging-First Workflow]] to upcoming videos and
   confirm a sustainable [[Publishing Cadence]].
5. Plan the funnel. Decide how [[Shorts]] will feed long form this month; see
   [[Shorts-to-Long-Form Funnel]].
6. Plan revenue. Identify one [[Diversify Revenue Streams]] action appropriate
   to the channel's tier.
7. Record rollback. For each change, note how to revert if it underperforms.

## Output

A one page roadmap: the focus bottleneck, three to five experiments with
hypotheses, the
packaging tests in flight, the cadence commitment, and the rollback notes. The
brain's
renderer can seed this from the scorecard.

## Cadence

Run the audit and refresh the roadmap monthly, or after every release for
monetization
sensitive channels. Review last month's hypotheses before setting new ones, so
the channel
learns.

## Guardrails

- Change few things at once so you can attribute results.
- Decide tests by watch time share and retention, not vanity views; see
  [[Chasing Views Over Satisfaction]].
- Keep every recommendation sourced and reversible.
