# Diversify Revenue Streams

Do not depend on ad revenue alone. Ad RPM swings with seasonality, niche, and
advertiser
demand, and the creator keeps only 55 percent of watch page ads and 45 percent
of the Shorts
pool. Fan funding pays a 70 percent share and does not depend on advertisers, so
a channel
that layers memberships, Supers, Shopping, and off platform income is both
higher earning
and more stable. This pattern operationalizes [[Revenue Streams]] and lifts
blended
[[RPM and CPM]].

## The pattern

1. Maximize the base. Lengthen high value videos past 8 minutes to unlock
   [[Mid-Roll Ads]] where the content earns it.
2. Add fan funding early. The lower [[YouTube Partner Program]] tier unlocks
   memberships, Super Chat, Super Thanks, and Shopping at 500 subscribers; the
   70 percent split is the best on platform rate.
3. Turn loyalty into recurring income. Memberships at up to six tiers reward
   your most engaged fans; see [[Community and Engagement]].
4. Tag products. The Shopping affiliate program is open at 500 subscribers with
   brand set commissions.
5. Build off platform. Sponsorships, your own products, and the Creator
   Partnerships marketplace often dwarf ad revenue for established creators.

## Why it works

A channel earning from five streams survives a bad ad quarter that would sink an
ads only
channel. Fan funding and Shopping also reward the loyal audience you build
through
[[Community and Engagement]], so diversification compounds with retention rather
than
competing with it.

## Sequencing by stage

- Pre monetization: build the audience and the [[Shorts-to-Long-Form Funnel]];
  revenue comes after eligibility.
- Lower YPP tier: turn on fan funding and Shopping immediately.
- Full YPP tier: optimize ads and [[Mid-Roll Ads]], then pursue sponsorships.

## Pitfalls

- Do not bolt on streams that annoy your audience; relevance matters more than
  count.
- Do not quote any split or threshold without checking the current figure; see
  [[Source Intake and Refresh]].
