# Packaging-First Workflow

Design the [[Titles]] and [[Thumbnails]] before you film, and only make videos
you can
package compellingly. This inverts the instinctive order, make the video then
figure out the
title, and it is the single most reliable production change a serious creator
can adopt.
Because [[Packaging]] sits at the top of the funnel and decides
[[Click-Through Rate]],
deciding it last means discovering too late that a good video has no compelling
promise.

## The pattern

1. Generate ideas as packages, not topics. Write the title and sketch the
   thumbnail for each idea.
2. Pressure test the package. If the title and thumbnail do not earn a click on
   their own, reconsider or drop the idea.
3. Plan the payoff. Decide how the video will deliver exactly what the package
   promises, protecting [[Audience Retention]].
4. Shoot for the package. Capture the specific thumbnail frames you need; do not
   rely on a screenshot.
5. Validate after publishing with a [[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]].

## Why it works

Top creators famously design the package before filming and scrap ideas that
cannot be
packaged. The logic is simple: a video the system never recommends because no
one clicks is
wasted effort, no matter how good it is. Packaging first forces the hardest
question, will
anyone choose this, to the front, where it is cheap to answer.

## Signals you are doing it right

- Your ideation document is full of title and thumbnail pairs, not bare topics.
- You sometimes kill ideas at the packaging stage, before filming.
- Your thumbnails use dedicated shots, not screenshots.
- Your title and thumbnail combine rather than repeat.

## Pitfalls

- Packaging first does not license dishonesty. The promise must be real, or you
  fall into [[Misleading Clickbait]] and break retention.
- Do not over polish the package for a video with no audience demand; weak
  impressions mean the topic, not the packaging, is the problem. See
  [[Studio Analytics]].

## How to apply

- Keep a running ideas board where every entry already has a working title and a
  rough thumbnail concept; promote only the ideas that earn a click on the board.
- Block packaging as its own step in the production calendar, ahead of scripting
  and filming, so a weak promise stops a video before it costs you a shoot.
- Turn the winners of each [[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]] into reusable package
  templates, while keeping every promise honest to protect
  [[Audience Retention]].
