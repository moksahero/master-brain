## The mental model

YouTube does not reward gaming a single number. It rewards satisfying a specific
audience.
The [[Recommendation System]] pulls a video toward the viewers most likely to be
satisfied,
weighting watch time by satisfaction. So the growth pillar reduces to a loop:
make a clear
promise with [[Packaging]], earn the click via [[Click-Through Rate]], keep the
promise via
[[Audience Retention]], and turn the resulting audience into
[[Revenue Streams]].

## The growth funnel

1. Reach: [[Discovery Surfaces]] and [[Hype]] decide who sees the thumbnail.
2. Click: [[Packaging]], [[Thumbnails]], and [[Titles]] convert impressions to
   views.
3. Watch: [[Hook in the First 30 Seconds]] and [[Audience Retention]] hold
   attention.
4. Continue: [[End Screens and Cards]] and [[Playlists and Series]] extend the
   session.
5. Return: [[Subscriptions and Subscribers]], [[Community and Engagement]], and
   [[Publishing Cadence]] build the habit.
6. Earn: [[YouTube Partner Program]], [[Mid-Roll Ads]], [[Shorts Monetization]],
   [[Channel Memberships]], [[Supers and Gifted Memberships]],
   [[YouTube Shopping and Product Tagging]], and [[Diversify Revenue Streams]]
   turn attention into income.

## Safety posture

- No credentials, tokens, API keys, or private audience data ever enter the
  brain.
- No monetization, growth, API, or ad claim without a current source; see
  [[Source Intake and Refresh]].
- No account mutation; the brain recommends, the operator acts.
- Avoid the antipatterns: [[Misleading Clickbait]],
  [[Bought Engagement and Sub4Sub]], [[Chasing Views Over Satisfaction]], and
  [[Inauthentic Mass-Produced Content]].
