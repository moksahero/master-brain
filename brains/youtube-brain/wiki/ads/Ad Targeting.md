# Ad Targeting

Targeting decides who sees a YouTube ad, and the modern reality is that audience
signals do
most of the work while privacy rules increasingly constrain what you can target
on. Building
a campaign on a restricted signal gets it disapproved, so the limits matter as
much as the
options. This pairs with [[Ad Bidding and Budget]] and the campaign wrappers in
[[Ad Campaign Types]].

## Audience segments

Google Ads offers several segment types:

- Affinity: long-term interests and habits.
- In-market: recent purchase intent.
- Life events: milestones like moving or graduating.
- Detailed demographics: durable life facts.
- Custom segments: built from keywords, URLs, and apps.
- Your-data segments (formerly remarketing, including Customer Match): people
  who already interacted with the business.

Video and Demand Gen campaigns layer demographics, keywords, topics, and
placements on top
of these audiences.

## Privacy and restricted targeting

Google never uses sensitive information such as health, race, religion, or
sexual
orientation to tailor ads. Advertisers in sensitive interest categories cannot
use
advertiser-curated audiences, which prevents inadvertent sensitive-interest
targeting. A
2025-12-12 policy update expanded Custom Segments for Display, broadening access
for
previously restricted health, finance, and regulated advertisers.

In the EU and EEA, Google's EU User Consent Policy requires consent collected
through a
consent-management platform, and the Digital Services Act bans targeting on
sensitive data
such as political views, religion, sexual orientation, and ethnicity. These
rules change
often; re-verify before advising, per [[Source Intake and Refresh]].

## Made-for-kids targeting

On content set as made for kids, YouTube serves no personalized ads, only
contextual ads
based on the video, as required by COPPA. This caps targeting and usually lowers
revenue;
see [[Made for Kids and COPPA]].

## How to apply

- Start from your-data and in-market segments for performance, affinity and
  demographics for reach.
- Confirm your industry is not in a restricted category before building
  audiences.
- For EU delivery, ensure consent-mode signals are in place.
- Measure which segments convert and prune the rest; see [[Ad Measurement]].
