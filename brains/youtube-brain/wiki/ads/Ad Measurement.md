# Ad Measurement

Measurement is how an advertiser separates what they paid for from what actually
happened,
and the key distinction is between a paid view and a downstream outcome.
Confusing the two
leads to claiming credit a campaign did not earn. This note covers the metrics;
the bid
models that produce them are in [[Ad Bidding and Budget]]. The organic,
creator-side
analytics live in [[Studio Analytics]].

## Paid versus earned versus view-through

- TrueView views are the paid views you are billed for.
- View-through conversions (VTC) happen when a user is served an impression that
  is not counted as a view or click, then later converts on the site.
- Earned actions (earned views, likes, subscribers, playlist adds, shares) are
  unpaid signals tracked within 7 days of the initial view, the advertising echo
  of organic [[Community and Engagement]].

## Awareness metrics

For awareness campaigns, Google Ads reports Unique Reach and Average Impression
Frequency,
quantifying how many distinct people saw the ad and how often. These pair with
the Target
frequency controls in [[Ad Bidding and Budget]].

## Lift studies

Brand Lift, Search Lift, and Conversion Lift run on the same campaign for
full-funnel
measurement. Advertisers select brand metrics such as ad recall, awareness,
consideration,
favorability, and purchase intent, with accelerated flights delivering
perception metrics
sooner and the ability to re-measure over time. Demand Gen's case for adding
image assets,
20 percent more conversions at the same CPA, is the kind of result these studies
surface;
see [[Ad Campaign Types]].

## Connecting to conversions

Conversion tracking and Google Ads reporting tie ad exposure to actions on the
advertiser's
site or app, while YouTube Analytics in Studio provides organic context. Reach
and frequency
reporting quantifies unique audience and exposure for the brand formats in
[[Connected TV and Shorts Ads]].

## How to apply

- Decide the objective first, then the metric: views for consideration,
  conversions or VTC for performance, lift for brand.
- Do not read TrueView views as outcomes; pair them with earned actions and
  conversions.
- Run a lift study for any meaningful brand budget, and re-measure rather than
  trusting a single flight.
