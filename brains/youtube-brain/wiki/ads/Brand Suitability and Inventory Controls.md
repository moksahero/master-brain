# Brand Suitability and Inventory Controls

Brand suitability is how an advertiser controls what kind of content their YouTube
ads run next to, trading reach against safety. It is the paid-side mirror of the
creator-facing [[Advertiser-Friendly Guidelines]]: creators decide what earns ad
revenue, advertisers decide where their ads are allowed to appear. The naming
changed recently, so this is also a common place for stale advice.

## Inventory modes (renamed)

Google Ads now names the three inventory modes Maximum, Moderate, and Limited.
These replace the older Expanded, Standard, and Limited labels, so any guidance
still using the old names is out of date.

- Maximum inventory: ads run across all available inventory for the widest reach,
  with only the most sensitive content auto-excluded.
- Moderate inventory: the balanced default; excludes strong profanity, dramatized
  violence, and sexually suggestive themes.
- Limited inventory: excludes as much sensitive content as possible, including
  moderate profanity and sexually suggestive themes, at the cost of reach.

Note that this Google Ads naming differs from Display and Video 360, which is
undergoing its own separate rename, so keep the two surfaces distinct.

## Content exclusions and labels

Beyond the inventory mode, advertisers can exclude specific content themes:
tragedy and conflict, sensitive social issues, profanity and rough language,
sexually suggestive content, and sensational and shocking. Digital content labels
(DL-G, DL-PG, DL-T, DL-MA, and not-yet-labeled) still exist, but as of September
2024 YouTube ads no longer use digital content labels from brand-safety settings;
labels now apply to Google Display Network inventory. This is a key accuracy point
when advising on YouTube specifically.

## Placement and channel exclusions

Advertisers can apply up to 65,000 placement exclusions across YouTube, Display,
video partners, and the Search Partner Network, blocking specific channels,
videos, apps, or sites. Exclusion lists are the precise tool when inventory modes
are too blunt, and they pair with the audience controls in [[Ad Targeting]].

## Where suitability now applies

As of November 2025, inventory types and excluded content themes extend to the
YouTube Home feed, the Watch Next feed, and Discover, covering Video Reach
Campaigns, Video View Campaigns, Performance Max, and Demand Gen
([[Ad Campaign Types]]). Existing settings auto-extend to these surfaces. Separately,
Parked Domains (AFD) stopped being a Search Partner surface on 2026-02-10, removing
that suitability option automatically.

## Suitability versus reach

Tighter suitability shrinks the available auction and can raise costs or limit
delivery, while looser settings widen reach but risk unwanted adjacencies. The
right setting depends on the brand's risk tolerance, not a universal default;
measure the delivery and outcome impact through [[Ad Measurement]] rather than
assuming the strictest setting is best.

## How to apply

- Start at Moderate inventory and tighten only where a specific brand risk
  justifies the reach loss.
- Use content-theme exclusions and placement exclusions for targeted control
  instead of dropping to Limited inventory wholesale.
- Remember digital content labels no longer gate YouTube ads; do not rely on them
  for YouTube brand safety.
- Re-verify suitability surfaces and naming periodically, since Google revises
  them often.
