# Discovery Surfaces

Discovery on YouTube is not one algorithm but several surfaces, each with its
own model and
its own weighting of signals. Treating "the algorithm" as monolithic leads to
bad decisions.
A creator should know which surface drives a given video and optimize for that
surface. The
traffic source report in [[Studio Analytics]] tells you which one is working.
The main
surfaces are Home (browse), Suggested videos, YouTube Search, the Shorts feed,
and channel
and notification traffic.

## Home and browse

Home is the personalized feed a logged in viewer sees. It optimizes for session
continuation
and satisfaction, drawing primarily on watch history. Home produces high
impression volume,
which naturally pulls [[Click-Through Rate]] down, so a lower CTR on browse
traffic is
normal and not a failure. Home rewards a strong, clear [[Packaging]] promise to
a broad but
relevant audience.

## Suggested videos

Suggested is the Up Next panel and the sidebar, traffic from suggestions next to
or after
other videos. It leans on the current video the viewer is watching, surfacing
videos
commonly watched together. Suggested is where "automated word of mouth" is most
visible: if
viewers of video A also watch your video B, the system links them. Strong
[[Audience Retention]] and topical adjacency drive Suggested.

## YouTube Search

Search ranks on three pillars: relevance, engagement, and quality. Relevance is
how well the
title, description, tags, and the video content match the query. Engagement for
search is
specifically the watch time of a video for that query. Quality reflects
expertise,
authoritativeness, and trustworthiness signals. Search yields fewer impressions
but higher
[[Click-Through Rate]] because intent is explicit, often 8 to 15 percent.
YouTube does not
accept payment for organic placement. See [[Titles]] for query matching.

## The Shorts feed

The Shorts feed is a decoupled model. It emphasizes the swipe versus watch
decision in the
first 1 to 3 seconds, replays and loops, and shares, and it weights metadata
less than long
form. The "Viewed vs swiped away" metric reports how often viewers watched
rather than
scrolled past. See [[Shorts]] and [[Audience Retention]].

## Reading the surfaces

- Open the traffic source report in [[Studio Analytics]]: Browse, Suggested,
  Search, External, Channel pages, Notifications, Playlists, Direct.
- Match your effort to your surface. A search video needs query-matched
  [[Titles]]; a browse video needs a bold [[Packaging]] promise; a Suggested
  video needs adjacency to popular videos.
- [[Hype]], [[Subscriptions and Subscribers|subscriptions]], and notifications
  give smaller channels extra discovery paths.
- Do not chase every surface at once; one clear promise to one audience travels
  furthest. See [[Recommendation System]].
