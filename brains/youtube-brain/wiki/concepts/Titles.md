# Titles

The title is the text half of [[Packaging]]. It works with the [[Thumbnails]] to
earn the
click and, on search traffic, it also matches the query. A good title front
loads the
important words, sparks curiosity without breaking the promise, and stays short
enough to
read at a glance. Title testing rolled out globally to creators around
2025-12-04, so titles
are now an A/B variable, not a one shot guess. See
[[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]].

## Length and structure

- Keep titles short, often under about 60 characters, so they are not truncated
  across surfaces; Backlinko's guidance is to use at least five words with the
  keyword near the front.
- Front load the most important and most searchable words; save episode numbers
  and branding for the end.
- Use ALL CAPS and emoji sparingly and deliberately, not as a default.

## Curiosity versus clarity

The title must balance two jobs that pull in opposite directions:

- On browse and Suggested, curiosity and stakes earn the click, but the
  curiosity has to be honest.
- On search, clarity and query match earn the click, because the viewer already
  knows what they want.

YouTube search ranks on relevance, engagement, and quality, and relevance reads
the title
against the query. A search focused video needs the query phrase near the front;
a browse
focused video can lead with the hook. Know your target [[Discovery Surfaces]]
before you
write.

## Combine, do not repeat

The title should not describe the thumbnail. If the thumbnail shows the emotion
or the
stakes, the title supplies the specific, the number, or the open loop. Redundant
packaging
wastes half the unit. See [[Packaging]].

## Honesty and policy

A title must accurately represent the video. Maliciously misleading titles, for
example
promising a full match and delivering a clip, violate the spam and deceptive
practices
policy and risk strikes and demonetization. A dishonest title also breaks
[[Audience Retention]] when the payoff never comes. See
[[Misleading Clickbait]].

## How to apply

- Draft three title options during [[Packaging-First Workflow]], before filming.
- Choose the structure for the [[Discovery Surfaces]] you expect: query forward
  for search, hook forward for browse.
- Run a Test and Compare title experiment; the winner is decided by watch time
  share.
- Read [[Click-Through Rate]] by traffic source in [[Studio Analytics]] to learn
  which titles convert.
