# Thumbnails

The thumbnail is the visual half of [[Packaging]] and the strongest single
driver of
[[Click-Through Rate]]. YouTube reports that 90 percent of the best performing
videos use a
custom thumbnail rather than an auto generated frame. A thumbnail has to read in
under a
second at small size on a phone, so clarity beats detail every time. Design it
before
filming so you can shoot the exact frame you need, and test it when the video
has enough
reach.

## Technical specs

- Recommended resolution 3840 by 2160, minimum width 640 pixels, 16:9 aspect
  ratio.
- Formats JPG, GIF, or PNG, under 2 MB on mobile (video) and up to 50 MB on
  desktop.
- The image renders at many sizes; check legibility at the smallest.

## Design principles that hold up

- Faces and clear emotion outperform object only frames. High intensity
  expressions beat polished static portraits, especially for younger viewers.
- High contrast and saturated color win; avoid YouTube's own white, red, and
  black UI colors so the thumbnail does not blend into the interface.
- Keep text minimal, often around 20 characters or fewer, in an easy to read
  font, so it is legible small.
- Use the rule of thirds and make the subject as large as the frame allows.
- Subscribers connect with familiar faces; casual viewers respond to universally
  relatable actions and emotions.

## Test, do not guess

Use the native Test and Compare feature to run up to three thumbnails. The
winner is chosen
by watch time share, which ties the thumbnail decision back to
[[Audience Retention]] rather
than raw clicks. Build a habit of testing on every video with sufficient
impressions. See
[[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]].

## Honesty

A thumbnail must represent the video. A thumbnail that promises a moment the
video never
delivers is malicious clickbait under policy and also destroys retention when
viewers feel
tricked. See [[Misleading Clickbait]] and [[Packaging]].

## How to apply

- Sketch two or three thumbnail concepts during ideation, before filming, as
  part of [[Packaging-First Workflow]].
- Shoot dedicated thumbnail frames; do not rely on a screenshot.
- Pair the thumbnail with a [[Titles]] choice that combines rather than repeats.
- Run Test and Compare and read [[Click-Through Rate]] by traffic source in
  [[Studio Analytics]].
