# Community Guidelines Strikes

Community Guidelines strikes are YouTube's enforcement track for content that
violates its
policies, and they are entirely separate from the copyright strikes covered in
[[Copyright Content ID and Strikes]]. The distinction matters because the two
systems have
different triggers, penalties, and remedies, and a creator who conflates them
will misjudge
the risk of a given action. This sits alongside the monetization rules in
[[Advertiser-Friendly Guidelines]].

## The graduated penalty model

- The first violation is typically a warning with no penalty. An optional policy
  training lets the warning expire after 90 days, unless the same policy is
  violated again within that window, which converts it into a strike.
- A first strike freezes posting for one week: no uploads, live streams, or
  scheduled public posts.
- A second strike within the same 90-day period extends the freeze to two weeks.
- Three strikes in the same 90-day period may permanently remove the channel.
  Each strike stays on the channel for 90 days.
- A single case of severe abuse can terminate a channel without warning.

Deleting the offending content does not remove a strike.

## What triggers a strike

Strikes come from violations of YouTube's content policies, for example violent
or graphic
content, harmful or dangerous content, spam and deception, and misinformation.
The
[[Misleading Clickbait]] and [[Inauthentic Mass-Produced Content]] antipatterns
describe
behaviors that risk both demonetization and strikes.

## The key distinction from copyright

The source is what separates the two systems: Community Guidelines strikes come
from policy
enforcement on content, whereas copyright strikes come from legal removal
requests by
rightsholders. They count separately, and a channel can be terminated by either
path. These
policies change; verify current wording before advising, per
[[Source Intake and Refresh]].

## How to apply

- Take the first warning seriously and complete the policy training so it can
  expire.
- Treat any strike as a serious signal and avoid a second within the 90-day
  window.
- Know which system a given risk falls under, because the remedies differ.
- Keep content within policy; deleting it after the fact does not undo a strike.
