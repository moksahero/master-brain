# Niche Selection and Positioning

A defined niche is the lever that lets the [[Recommendation System]] work for a
channel. The
system recognizes a viewer's favored themes, topics, and formats and models what
is popular
among similar audiences, so consistent topical focus lets it confidently cluster
a channel
with a defined audience. Scattered, unrelated uploads weaken that signal. Niche
is the
highest-leverage decision a new creator makes, and it sets both the growth path
and the
revenue ceiling.

## Why focus helps discovery

YouTube's guidance is to focus on what your audience likes, and the
recommendations will
follow. A channel that repeatedly serves one audience builds the "viewers of A
also watched
B" adjacency that surfaces it in Suggested and Home; see [[Discovery Surfaces]]
and
[[Audience Overlap]]. A channel that jumps topics gives the system no stable
audience to
match.

## Niche sets the revenue ceiling

Niche choice also caps monetization. Finance, insurance, and legal niches
command the
highest CPMs, roughly 15 to 50 dollars, while gaming and entertainment sit near
1 to 8
dollars, and creator RPM typically lands 30 to 50 percent below CPM after
YouTube's share.
See [[RPM and CPM]]. The strategic tradeoff is real: high-RPM niches are narrow
and
competitive, while broad entertainment niches reach scale but monetize thinly.

## Positioning

Positioning is how the niche is expressed: the specific angle, audience, and
promise that
distinguish the channel from others in the same space. A clear position makes
the
[[Channel Page and Branding]] trailer easy to write and the
[[Ideation and Inspiration]]
pipeline obvious.

## How to apply

- Narrow far enough that the topical match is unambiguous, so the system can
  cluster you cleanly.
- Broaden only along audience-adjacent themes the same viewers already watch,
  preserving the session-continuation logic; confirm adjacency in
  [[Audience Overlap]].
- Weigh the RPM ceiling against competition when picking between a high-value
  narrow niche and a broad one.
- Express the position in the channel trailer, the [[Publishing Cadence]], and
  every package.
