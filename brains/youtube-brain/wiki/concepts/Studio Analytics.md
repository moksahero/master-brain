# Studio Analytics

YouTube Studio Analytics is the creator's instrument panel, organized into tabs:
Overview,
Content, Reach, Engagement, Audience, Revenue, and Trends. Reading it well means
following
one funnel from impression to subscriber and knowing which metric points to
which fix. The
most common mistake is reading a single number in isolation; every metric is
meaningful only
against your own typical performance and the relevant traffic source. This
concept ties
together [[Click-Through Rate]], [[Watch Time and AVD]], and
[[Audience Retention]].

## The tabs

- Reach: impressions, impressions CTR, views, unique viewers, and traffic
  sources. The top of the funnel.
- Engagement: watch time, average view duration, end screen and card click
  rates, top videos.
- Audience: returning versus new viewers, the rolling 28 day monthly audience,
  subscriber watch-time share, when your viewers are on YouTube, and which other
  channels and videos they watch.
- Revenue: estimated revenue, RPM, and the streams from [[Revenue Streams]].
- Trends: what audiences are searching for.

## Traffic sources

The traffic source report tells you which [[Discovery Surfaces]] is working:
Browse
features, Suggested videos, YouTube search, Channel pages, External,
Notifications,
Playlists, and Direct or unknown. A registered impression counts only when a
thumbnail shows
for more than one second with at least 50 percent visible. Match your
optimization to your
dominant source.

## The funnel as a diagnostic chain

The official funnel runs impressions, to CTR, to views, to watch time, to
subscribers
driven. Read it as a chain of fixes:

- Weak impressions: the topic or demand is the problem, not the packaging.
- Strong impressions, weak CTR: fix the [[Thumbnails]] and [[Titles]].
- Strong CTR, weak AVD: the hook or pacing is weak; see
  [[Hook in the First 30 Seconds]].
- Strong AVD, few returning viewers: the content satisfies but does not build a
  habit; work on series and [[Publishing Cadence]].

YouTube warns that high CTR with low AVD, classic clickbait, backfires. See
[[Misleading Clickbait]].

## How to apply

- Always read a metric against your own 28 day average and the traffic source,
  never in isolation.
- Use the funnel to localize the problem before changing anything.
- Run the [[Channel Health Audit]] flow monthly and feed it into the
  [[Monthly Optimization Roadmap]].
