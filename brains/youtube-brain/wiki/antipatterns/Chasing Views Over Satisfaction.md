# Chasing Views Over Satisfaction

Optimizing for raw views or raw watch minutes, instead of viewer satisfaction,
is the
strategic antipattern that the modern [[Recommendation System]] is specifically
built to
defeat. YouTube weights watch time by satisfaction, measured through surveys,
returns, and
session continuation. Tactics that inflate a vanity number while lowering
satisfaction
therefore backfire: the system reads the dissatisfaction and stops promoting the
content.

## Forms of the antipattern

- Padding videos to raise [[Watch Time and AVD]]. YouTube explicitly says to
  avoid filler and artificial stretching; padding raises minutes while lowering
  satisfaction.
- Posting high volume low value content to game [[Publishing Cadence]].
  Frequency helps only when quality holds.
- Farming shallow [[Shorts]] views that never convert, ignoring that Shorts RPM
  is near zero and the value is in the funnel; see [[Shorts Monetization]] and
  [[Shorts-to-Long-Form Funnel]].
- Engagement bait that asks for likes and comments without earning them, which
  manufactures a hollow signal.

## Why it fails

The 2024 to 2026 system measures how viewers feel about the time they spend, not
just the
minutes. YouTube's Todd Beaupre describes weighting watch time by satisfaction
signals. The
blog that introduced watch time also introduced valued watchtime via 1 to 5 star
surveys
where only 4 and 5 star ratings count. A padded or baited video can win the
short term
number and lose the satisfaction signal that actually drives sustained reach.

## The honest alternative

Make each video as long as it deserves and no longer, keep the [[Packaging]]
promise, and
build the returning audience through real [[Community and Engagement]]. Judge
success by
satisfaction proxies: [[Audience Retention]], returning viewers, and watch time
from
subscribers in [[Studio Analytics]], not by a single vanity view count.

## How to avoid it

- Ask of every choice: does this make the viewer more satisfied, or just the
  number bigger.
- Cut anything that raises minutes but lowers the experience.
- Track returning viewers and retention, not just views.
