# Misleading Clickbait

Misleading clickbait is [[Packaging]] that promises something the video does not
deliver. It
fails twice: it violates YouTube policy, and it destroys [[Audience Retention]]
when viewers
feel tricked. The distinction that matters is between a strong, honest hook and
a deceptive
one. Curiosity and bold framing are good; a title or thumbnail that promises a
payoff the
video never provides is the antipattern.

## Why it fails

- Policy: maliciously misleading titles, thumbnails, descriptions, or imagery
  violate the spam, deceptive practices, and scams policy. Consequences include
  removal, strikes, monetization suspension, and termination after three strikes
  in 90 days. See [[Advertiser-Friendly Guidelines]].
- Retention: a click earned by a false promise is followed by an immediate exit,
  which craters the retention curve. High [[Click-Through Rate]] with low
  retention is the signature of clickbait, and the [[Recommendation System]]
  stops promoting such videos.
- Trust: tricked viewers do not return, so even a one time view spike costs you
  the returning audience that compounds growth.

## The honest alternative

The native Test and Compare feature chooses the winning package by watch time
share, not raw
clicks, precisely to reward honest packaging. The fix is not weaker packaging;
it is a
package whose promise the video genuinely keeps. See
[[Title-Thumbnail A-B Test Loop]] and
[[Packaging-First Workflow]].

## Telltale signs you have crossed the line

- The thumbnail shows a moment that never appears in the video.
- The title asks a question the video does not answer, or implies a result it
  does not show.
- Your CTR is high but your 30 second retention and AVD are low.
- Comments say "thumbnail lied" or "where was the part from the title."

## How to avoid it

- Make the title and thumbnail a promise you can keep, then keep it in the first
  30 seconds; see [[Hook in the First 30 Seconds]].
- If a bold package cannot be delivered honestly, change the video, not just the
  package.
- Watch the gap between CTR and retention in [[Studio Analytics]]; a wide gap is
  a warning.
