# 🧹 Edit & QA

> Catch the problems before the client does. A short, repeatable review every deliverable passes before it ships.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/chat-cf-edit.gif)

*Ask how to keep weak work from shipping and Claude pulls the five-box QA check.*

---

## The QA checklist (run on every deliverable)

- **True.** Every stat and claim is real and sourced. AI invents confident nonsense. Verify it.
- **On-brand.** Voice matches the client. No hype, no filler, sounds like them.
- **On-target.** It serves the "win" you pinned in Plan, not just word count.
- **Technically clean.** Links work, schema is valid, headings make sense.
- **Human-checked.** You read it start to finish. Not just skimmed.

---

## Let the tools help you check

- `/blog` quality gates flag thin or weak sections before you do.
- `/seo content <url>` re-scores a page after edits so you see the lift.
- Fact-check pass: `Verify every statistic and claim in content/<file> and flag anything you can't source.`

---

## The one rule

**Nothing ships unread.** The fastest way to lose a client is one fabricated stat or one off-brand sentence with their name on it. The review is cheap. The cleanup isn't.

**You're done when:** the deliverable passes all five boxes and you'd happily put your own name on it.

> 💡 Build a tiny `qa.md` in the client folder with these five boxes. Tick them per deliverable. It turns "I think it's fine" into "it passed."

> ✅ Run the checklist on the thing you drafted last lesson. Post the one issue it caught. (There's almost always one.)

- Edit is the step people skip and the one that protects your name.
- Five boxes: true, on-brand, on-target, technically clean, human-checked.
- Nothing ships unread. Use the quality gates, but you make the final call.
