# 📈 Improve: Report & Retainer

> Show the client what changed, catch anything slipping, and tee up next month so the retainer renews itself.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/chat-cf-report.gif)

*Ask how to report results and Claude pulls the commands that make a retainer renew.*

---

## Show the change (your after photo)

Remember the baseline audit from Analyze? Compare against it.

```text
/seo google report full        (rankings, traffic, standing, from real Search Console data)
/seo drift compare <url>        (did anything slip since last time?)
```

Put the before (Analyze) next to the after (now). That contrast *is* the report. Save to `reports/`.

---

## Make the report client-readable

Clients don't read scorecards. They read outcomes.

```text
Turn reports/<file> into a one-page client update: what we did, what moved
(tie it to their 90-day "win"), and the 3 things we'll do next month.
```

Lead with their pinned "win" number. "Booked calls from search up from 4 to 11" beats a wall of metrics.

---

Set the next cycle (the retainer)

Improve feeds the next Plan. The "3 things next month" *is* next month's scope. That's the loop:

**Plan → Analyze → Draft → Edit → Improve → (next) Plan.**

A client who sees a clear monthly update tied to their goal renews without being asked. The flow sells itself.

**You're done when:** the client has a one-page update tied to their win, and next month's 3 priorities are set.

> 💡 Run `/seo drift` on a schedule. Catching a slip before the client notices is what separates a retainer from a one-off.

> ✅ Generate one report (`/seo google report full`) and post the single number you'd put at the top of a client update.

- Compare the after (`/seo google report full`, `/seo drift`) against the Analyze baseline.
- Report outcomes tied to the pinned "win," not raw metrics. One page.
- "3 things next month" becomes next month's Plan. The loop is the retainer.
