# 📋 Plan: Scope the Client

> Take a new client from "hello" to a scoped, ready-to-work project folder, without a 10-page proposal.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/chat-cf-scope.gif)

## SOP: Scope the client

**What you're building:** a client folder and a one-page scope. **Before you start:** the client's website URL and what they sell. **Steps:**

1. **Make the folder.** `~/Clients/<client-name>` and drop your `.claude/settings.json` in it (Course 2). Open it in [Claude Code](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/708c1575?md=f579fae5e640447f90c73fb4ee8ed75f).
2. **Answer four questions** in `notes.md`:

- What do they sell, and to whom?
- What does "win" mean in 90 days? (Leads? Calls? Ranked pages?)
- Who are their 3 real competitors?
- What access do you have? (Site, Google Business Profile, Search Console, ad accounts.)

1. **Get the access you need.** Search Console + GA4 at minimum for SEO; ad account access for paid. Ask on day one.
2. **Pick the path.** Rank, Publish, or Advertise (from Start Here). Most clients start at Rank.

**You're done when:** the folder exists, `notes.md` has the four answers, and you know your first command.

> 💡 Vague scope makes vague work. "Win" = "15 booked calls a month from search" beats "more traffic." Pin the number.

## The intake prompt

```text
I'm scoping a new client: <name>, a <type of business> in <city/region> that sells <offer>.
Their 90-day goal is <goal>. Ask me the 5 questions you most need answered to plan their
SEO + content, then summarize the scope in one page I can save to notes.md.
```

> ✅ Scope one client (real or practice) into a folder with `notes.md`. Post the one-line "win" you pinned.

- Make the folder first, then scope in `notes.md`: offer, win, competitors, access.
- Pin a number for "win." Vague goals make vague work.
- Get Search Console + GA4 access on day one.
