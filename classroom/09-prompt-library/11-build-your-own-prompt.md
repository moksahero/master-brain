# 🛠️ Build Your Own Prompt

> Stop hunting for the perfect prompt and write your own. Four parts make any prompt reliable: role, context, task, format. Then you test it and save the winners into your own library.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/chat-pl-byo.gif)

*Four parts make any prompt reliable, then you save the winners.*

---

### The four parts

Every strong prompt has four parts. Miss one and quality drops.

- **Role.** Who Claude should be. "You are a senior PPC strategist."
- **Context.** Your business, your audience, your constraints.
- **Task.** The one thing you want done, with a verb. "Write 5 headlines."
- **Format.** Exactly how the answer should be shaped. A table. Five bullets. 90 words.

Format is the most-skipped and highest-leverage part. Skip it and you get mush.

---

Here it is, all four parts in one prompt:

```text
Role: You are a conversion copywriter for B2B SaaS.
Context: We sell <product> to <audience>. Our edge is <differentiator>.
Task: Write 5 homepage hero headlines.
Format: A numbered list, each headline under 10 words, with a one-line reason for each.
```

**Optional fifth part, constraints.** Once the four parts work, add a line for what to avoid. It sharpens the output fast.

```text
Constraints: No buzzwords, no "revolutionary", lead with the customer's outcome.
```

### Test, then tighten

A first draft rarely nails it. Don't start over. Tell Claude what was wrong and what to fix. One move:

```text
That output was <too long / too generic / off-voice>. Redo it, and this time <the specific fix>. Keep everything else.
```

"That was too generic. Rewrite assuming the reader has already tried three competitors" fixes more than a full rewrite.

### Save what works

When a prompt produces gold, save it. Keep one note (an Obsidian file works) with your best prompts and the brackets already marked. Next month you copy, swap, run. Your own private library that beats any list someone else wrote. That's the same pattern this whole course is built on.

---

- Save winning prompts in one note with brackets marked. Build your own library.
