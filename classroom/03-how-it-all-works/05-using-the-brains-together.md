# 🧠 Using the Brains Together

> **What you'll be able to do:** Use [Marketing Brain](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=888db513f2b24f4d85320fe80ee88bbb) and [Local SEO Brain](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=22902679826f428295b66ca4141160bd) on the same project, in the right order, so they add up instead of confusing you.

This is the question that came up most: *"How do I use the Marketing Brain with the Local Brain together?"* Here's the clean answer.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/c3-brains-merge.gif)

### They do different jobs. That's why you use both.

- [**Marketing Brain**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=888db513f2b24f4d85320fe80ee88bbb) = the **research and strategy** engine. Its 6-step [DataForSEO](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/2afc71bf?md=af828c1dd05c40aeb37ff7fe01730f13) pipeline finds the demand, the competitors, and the keyword map. Output: a strategy plan.
- [**Local SEO Brain**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=22902679826f428295b66ca4141160bd) = the **local execution** playbook. 59 chapters covering Google Business Profile, citations/NAP, local content, maps. Output: the local moves to make.

One finds *what to go after*. The other tells you *how to win it locally*. No overlap. A relay.

---

### The order (this is the whole trick)

1. [**Marketing Brain**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=888db513f2b24f4d85320fe80ee88bbb)** first.** Run the research pipeline for the client's niche + city. You get the keyword/competitor map and the strategy.
2. **Hand it to **[**Local SEO Brain**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=22902679826f428295b66ca4141160bd)**.** Take that strategy in as the input, and work the relevant local chapters (GBP, citations, local pages) against it.
3. **Skills execute.** `/seo local`, `/seo maps`, `/blog write` do the actual tasks, reading from both vaults.

Research → local plan → execution. Same client, one direction.

---

### How they share a client

Both Brains live in `~/AI-Marketing-Hub` (Drawer 2). The **client's** work lives in its own folder (Drawer 3). You point [Claude Code](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/708c1575?md=f579fae5e640447f90c73fb4ee8ed75f) at the client folder and tell it which Brain vaults to read. The bridge is a prompt:

```text
Read my Marketing Brain strategy for <client> (the keyword + competitor map) and my
Local SEO Brain chapters on Google Business Profile, citations, and local content.
Produce one local SEO action plan for <client> in <city>: what to fix first,
the GBP changes, the citations to build, and 3 local pages to write.
```

> The full, refined "merge the Brains" prompt lives in [the Prompt Library](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/2afc71bf?md=28cc74b0a0c246208b5df5073fa1f3bf). Copy it from there.

### When to add [Ads Brain](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/c1d53bcf?md=9166b414941a45cb851448ef0c5ee7ac)

Once the local plan is producing ranked, converting pages, **Ads Brain** layers paid on top of the winners. Same relay: organic strategy first, paid amplification after.
