# 🗺️ Skills vs Brains vs Apps vs Codex

> Look at any product in the Hub and instantly know what kind it is and when you'd use it. This is the lesson that ends the "they all overlap" feeling.

![](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/AI-Marketing-Hub/classroom-assets@cfc5a1e99148d256bff96912f16ee28322523f56/c3-decision-matrix.gif)

- [**Skills**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/708c1575?md=c4ccd34fe8754432a44496f89985917e)** = do.** Run a task right now.
- [**Brains**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/708c1575?md=267ca6db814347ebb54fc1e1de033998)** = remember.** Hold research and strategy so work compounds.
- [**Codex**](https://www.skool.com/ai-marketing-hub-pro/classroom/708c1575?md=d1ba646701774675a4fa5032e757dfb7)** = same, different engine.**

---

A real example, one local client:

1. **Brain** holds the research and strategy (Marketing Brain ran the keyword + competitor pipeline).
2. **Skill** does the task (`/seo audit`, `/blog write`) reading from that Brain.

Same job, two roles. Not two competing products. You don't pick *between* them. You use the ones the job needs.
